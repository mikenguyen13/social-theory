4.6 Glass Cliff Effect

This effect is when woemn are more likely to be appointed to a more challenging position (poorly performing orgs), while men are like more likely to be appointed to a stable leadership position (Ryan and Haslam 2005; Ryan et al. 2016)

References

Ryan, Michelle K., and S. Alexander Haslam. 2005. “The Glass Cliff: Evidence That Women Are over-Represented in Precarious Leadership Positions.” British Journal of Management 16 (2): 81–90. https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1467-8551.2005.00433.x.

Ryan, Michelle K., S. Alexander Haslam, Thekla Morgenroth, Floor Rink, Janka Stoker, and Kim Peters. 2016. “Getting on Top of the Glass Cliff: Reviewing a Decade of Evidence, Explanations, and Impact.” The Leadership Quarterly 27 (3): 446–55. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.leaqua.2015.10.008.
